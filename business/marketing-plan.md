# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
"Pay-Tracker: Simplifying payment status tracking and collections for developers and businesses."

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**  
1. "Streamline your billing process with Pay-Tracker, the ultimate tool for managing complex invoice payments."
2. "Stay on top of your finances with Pay-Tracker, designed to simplify payment tracking and enhance cash flow management."
3. "Transform your invoicing experience with Pay-Tracker, making payment status visibility effortless for developers and businesses alike."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Alex the Freelance Developer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Manages multiple client projects, sends invoices, and tracks payments across different platforms.  
   - **Budget:** $50/month for billing tools.

2. **Persona 2: Jamie the Small Business Owner**  
   - **Daily Job:** Oversees business operations, handles invoicing, and follows up on overdue payments.  
   - **Budget:** $100/month for financial management solutions.

3. **Persona 3: Morgan the Finance Manager at a Startup**  
   - **Daily Job:** Responsible for financial reporting, managing cash flow, and ensuring timely collections from clients.  
   - **Budget:** $200/month for comprehensive billing and invoicing systems.

## Channels
1. **Content Marketing (Blog & SEO)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $30 per customer acquired through organic traffic and content engagement.

2. **Social Media Advertising (LinkedIn & Facebook)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $50 per customer acquired through targeted ads focusing on developers and small businesses.

3. **Partnerships with Accounting Software Providers**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $100 per customer acquired through referral programs and integrations.

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Launch blog post introducing Pay-Tracker, highlighting key features and benefits.
- **Week 2:** Create a case study showcasing a successful use of Pay-Tracker by a beta user.
- **Week 3:** Develop an infographic on the importance of payment tracking for businesses.
- **Week 4:** Host a webinar on best practices for invoicing and payment collections.
- **Week 5:** Release a video tutorial on how to use Pay-Tracker effectively.
- **Week 6:** Publish a blog post on common billing mistakes and how to avoid them.
- **Week 7:** Share testimonials from early adopters on social media.
- **Week 8:** Create a comparison chart showing Pay-Tracker vs. competitors.
- **Week 9:** Launch a targeted email campaign to potential users highlighting new features.
- **Week 10:** Post a Q&A session on social media addressing user questions about Pay-Tracker.
- **Week 11:** Release a whitepaper on the future of billing and invoicing technology.
- **Week 12:** Recap the journey of Pay-Tracker and share user success stories.

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and pricing strategy; initiate beta testing with select users.
- **D-0:** Official product launch; activate marketing campaigns across chosen channels.
- **D+30:** Analyze initial user feedback; implement necessary adjustments; start gathering testimonials.
- **D+90:** Evaluate performance against success metrics; prepare for potential feature updates based on user feedback.

## Success Metrics
- **DAU (Daily Active Users):** Target 500 DAU by D+90.
- **$MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) Target:** Aim for $10,000 in MRR by D+90.
